# The Path to Senior

Senior is one of the most overloaded words in engineering careers. It sounds like a reward for surviving long enough, but teams usually recognize seniority through a different pattern: better problem framing, better trade-off calls, and more leverage created for other people.

This is the final post in the Developer Career 101 series.

## Questions this chapter answers

- What actually separates a senior engineer from a mid-level engineer?
- How do problem framing, judgment, influence, and mentoring show up in day-to-day work?
- Which behaviors matter beyond technical depth as your scope expands?
- What can you start practicing now, before the title changes?

> Seniority is less about doing more tickets yourself and more about improving the quality of decisions around you.

## What You Will Learn

- The definition of *senior*
- Technical *depth*
- *Influence*
- *Mentoring*
- *Continuous* learning

## Why It Matters

Senior is a behavior, not a title.

## Key Terms

- **scope**: Range of responsibility.
- **impact**: Effect created.
- **judgment**: Decision quality.
- **ownership**: Ownership of outcomes.
- **multiplier**: Force multiplier of teammates.

## Before/After

**Before**: "I just close tickets."

**After**: "I define problems and propose solution directions."

## Hands-on: Five Senior Behaviors

### Step 1 — Define the Problem

```text
Write the "problem", not the "symptom".
```

### Step 2 — Decision Records

```markdown
- decision
- alternatives
- rationale
- impact
```

### Step 3 — Mentor One Person

```text
- monthly 1:1
- mentee sets the agenda
```

### Step 4 — Five-Line Review Rule

```text
- 1 praise
- 1 risk
- 1 alternative
- 1 learning point
- 1 next step
```

### Step 5 — Learning Retro

```bash
# write a 30-minute retro every quarter
```

## A practical ladder toward senior behavior

| Stage | Typical focus | Move that unlocks the next level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Junior | Solve assigned problems reliably | Ask more context questions and run retros |
| Mid-level | Structure work independently | Document alternatives and reasons for decisions |
| Senior | Define important problems and set priorities | Raise the quality of team habits and other engineers |
| Beyond | Shape org-level systems and standards | Build repeatable mechanisms, not one-off heroics |

## What to Notice in This Code

- Documents amplify influence.
- Mentoring is a multiplier.
- Retros compound.

## Five Common Mistakes

1. **Going deep on tech only.**
2. **Skipping decision documents.**
3. **Aggressive code reviews.**
4. **Postponing mentoring.**
5. **Skipping retros.**

## How This Shows Up in Production

Senior tracks at large companies extend into Staff and Principal levels.

## How a Senior Engineer Thinks

- Expand scope.
- Measure impact.
- Record judgment.
- Grow peers.
- Never stop learning.

## Checklist

- [ ] Problem-definition habit.
- [ ] Decision document template.
- [ ] One mentee.
- [ ] Quarterly retro.
