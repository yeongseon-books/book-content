# The Case Interview

> Data Science Career 101 series (7/10)

<!-- a-grade-intro:begin -->

**Core question**: How do you answer a *case interview*?

> Clarify the problem, pick the metric, form hypotheses, use data, decide.

<!-- a-grade-intro:end -->

## What You Will Learn

- Case *types*
- An answer *frame*
- Metric *design*
- Hypothesis *testing*
- Decision *proposal*

## Why It Matters

Tech without product sense fails the loop.

## Concept at a Glance

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    Q[Clarify] --> M[Metric]
    M --> H[Hypothesis]
    H --> D[Data]
    D --> Dec[Decision]
```

## Key Terms

- **product sense**: Sense of product impact.
- **north star metric**: Top-level metric.
- **hypothesis**: A testable explanation.
- **A/B test**: Controlled experiment.
- **trade-off**: Competing goals.

## Before/After

**Before**: "I just throw out numbers."

**After**: "I follow Problem to Metric to Hypothesis to Data to Decision."

## Hands-on: Five-Step Frame

### Step 1 — Clarify

```text
"DAU dropped" = which segment, what period?
```

### Step 2 — Metric

```text
- north star metric
- two supporting metrics
```

### Step 3 — Hypothesis

```text
- product change
- external event
- data pipeline
```

### Step 4 — Data

```text
- which query, which comparison
- A/B feasibility
```

### Step 5 — Decision

```text
- recommended action
- risks
- follow-up measurement
```

## What to Notice in This Code

- Clarifying buys time.
- The metric anchors the conversation.
- Always reach a decision.

## Five Common Mistakes

1. **Answering immediately.**
2. **No metric defined.**
3. **Single hypothesis.**
4. **Vague data proposal.**
5. **Ending without a decision.**

## How This Shows Up in Production

More than half of big tech PM/scientist interviews are case-style.

## How a Senior Engineer Thinks

- Clarify first.
- Pick a north star.
- Three hypotheses.
- Concrete data plan.
- Close with a decision.

## Checklist

- [ ] Three clarifying questions.
- [ ] One metric plus supports.
- [ ] Three hypotheses.
- [ ] One decision.

## Practice Problems

1. One line: define north star metric.
2. One line: example of a trade-off.
3. One line: criterion for a clarifying question.
