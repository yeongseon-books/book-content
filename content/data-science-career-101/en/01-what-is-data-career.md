# What Is a Data Career

> Data Science Career 101 series (1/10)

<!-- a-grade-intro:begin -->

**Core question**: What does a *data career* actually look like as a job?

> Answer questions and improve products with data.

<!-- a-grade-intro:end -->

## What You Will Learn

- The data career *landscape*
- Five major *roles*
- Core *skills*
- Entry *paths*
- Common *misconceptions*

## Why It Matters

The same data carries different expectations across roles.

## Concept at a Glance

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    Q[Question] --> D[Data]
    D --> A[Analysis]
    A --> P[Product]
```

## Key Terms

- **data analyst**: Decision support specialist.
- **data scientist**: Hypothesis tester.
- **data engineer**: Pipeline builder.
- **ML engineer**: Model operator.
- **analytics engineer**: Reliable analytics modeler.

## Before/After

**Before**: "I thought data work meant statistics only."

**After**: "I can distinguish five role expectations."

## Hands-on: One-Liner per Role

### Step 1 — Analyst

```text
Drives decisions with SQL and dashboards.
```

### Step 2 — Scientist

```text
Tests hypotheses with experiments and models.
```

### Step 3 — Engineer

```text
Builds pipelines and storage as data infrastructure.
```

### Step 4 — ML Engineer

```text
Trains, deploys, and monitors models.
```

### Step 5 — Analytics Engineer

```text
Builds reliable analytics models with dbt and friends.
```

## What to Notice in This Code

- Look at responsibilities, not titles.
- Definitions vary by company.
- Boundaries blur in practice.

## Five Common Mistakes

1. **Judging by job title alone.**
2. **Learning every tool at once.**
3. **Memorizing tools without context.**
4. **Ignoring the business domain.**
5. **Skipping measurement.**

## How This Shows Up in Production

Big tech separates Analyst, Scientist, ML Engineer, and Analytics Engineer.

## How a Senior Engineer Thinks

- Question first.
- Data is the means.
- Measurement is evidence.
- Domain creates leverage.
- Connection creates impact.
